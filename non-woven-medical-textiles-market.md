<p><p>The non-woven medical textiles market is categorized into several types, each serving unique applications. Meltblown textiles are known for their filtration properties, making them ideal for masks and surgical gowns. Spunbonded fabrics are durable and lightweight, commonly used in drapes and other medical coverings. Spunlace textiles offer soft texture suitable for wipes and dressings. Needle punch fabrics provide structural integrity for sterile applications. Other types may include composites or specialized textiles tailored for specific medical needs, enhancing versatility in healthcare settings.</p></p>
