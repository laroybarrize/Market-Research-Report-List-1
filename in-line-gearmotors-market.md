<p><p>In-line gearmotors are categorized into two primary types based on their power source: AC and DC. AC gearmotors are typically used in industrial applications due to their efficiency and durability, often operating at constant speeds. They are ideal for applications requiring continuous operation. Conversely, DC gearmotors offer variable speed control and are more suited for applications that demand precision and flexibility, such as robotics and automotive systems. Both types play crucial roles across diverse industries, enhancing productivity and performance.</p></p>
