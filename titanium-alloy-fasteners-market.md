<p><p>The titanium alloy fasteners market is primarily divided into threaded and non-threaded fasteners. Threaded fasteners, such as screws and bolts, feature helical ridges for secure connections, commonly used in various industries for their strength and lightweight properties. Non-threaded fasteners, including pins and clips, provide stability without the need for threading, ideal for applications requiring quick assembly and disassembly. Both types leverage titanium's corrosion resistance and high strength-to-weight ratio, making them essential in aerospace, automotive, and military sectors.</p></p>
