<p><p>PU resins for artificial leather are primarily categorized into two market types: dry-process synthetic leather and wet-process synthetic leather. The dry-process method involves applying PU resins directly on a substrate, resulting in a thinner, more flexible material, ideal for applications such as clothing and upholstery. In contrast, the wet-process method utilizes a polymer solution to create thicker, more durable artificial leather, often used in automotive interiors and high-wear applications. Each method caters to distinct market needs and performance characteristics.</p></p>
